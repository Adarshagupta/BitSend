BitSend as a local-first payment system

A product and system view of how the app, nearby transfer layer, receipts, and later settlement fit together.

Back to Research Read Product Overview
System framing

The architecture follows the real-world payment sequence.

BitSend is not just a wallet with extra transport options. The product is built around a different sequence: find the other phone, move the payment, then let the online system complete the durable chain and receipt steps after the fact.

Main components

App, nearby layer, backend services, and receipt storage.

The mobile app carries the user-facing flow. Nearby transport paths support local exchange. Backend services help with online settlement and receipt persistence. The full system only makes sense when those pieces are treated as one product, not as disconnected modules.

  • The app owns the user interaction and trust cues.
  • The nearby layer supports the in-person send moment.
  • The backend helps when final network work is needed.
  • The receipt layer preserves clarity after the transfer.
Product consequence

Local-first changes how success is defined.

In a normal crypto flow, success is often defined by immediate on-chain confirmation. In BitSend, the more important success event is whether the people involved finished the nearby exchange cleanly and trust what happened. Final chain state still matters, but it supports that earlier outcome.
