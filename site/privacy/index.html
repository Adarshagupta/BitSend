Privacy policy

How BitSend handles device and network data.

Effective date: March 20, 2026. BitSend is designed as a non-custodial mobile wallet app. This page explains what the app stores on-device, what it sends to online endpoints when needed, and how privacy questions can be raised.

Read Terms Privacy Contact

Overview

This policy applies to BitSend users and to related backend endpoints used by the app. By using BitSend, you agree to this privacy policy.

Information stored on your device

BitSend may store local app data such as:

  • Wallet metadata and wallet addresses.
  • Local transfer queue and activity history.
  • App settings, endpoint selections, and preferences.
  • Security state used for unlock and biometric checks.

Sensitive secrets are intended to stay on-device and use platform security features where available.

Permissions and device access

BitSend may ask for permissions when a feature needs them:

  • Camera for QR address and transfer scans.
  • Nearby devices and Bluetooth for local discovery and transfer.
  • Microphone for ultrasonic receive workflows.
  • Network and Wi-Fi state for connectivity-aware behavior.
  • Biometric capability for device authentication.

Network requests

When you use connected or managed features, BitSend may send request data to blockchain RPC endpoints, optional managed backend endpoints, or optional relay and receipt endpoints.

  • Wallet addresses and chain or network parameters.
  • Signed payloads needed to complete operations.
  • Request metadata required for online settlement.

How information is used

  • Operate wallet, transfer, receive, and settlement features.
  • Authenticate access to app functions.
  • Maintain local history, queue, and settings.
  • Troubleshoot failures and improve reliability.
  • Meet security, fraud-prevention, or legal obligations when required.

Data sharing and retention

BitSend does not sell personal data.

  • Data may be shared with infrastructure providers you use.
  • Optional backend and receipt providers may process request data.
  • Legal authorities may receive data when required by law.
  • On-device data remains until you remove it or uninstall the app.
  • External endpoint retention follows the policies of those operators.

Security, rights, and updates

BitSend applies technical safeguards, but no system is fully secure. Users remain responsible for securing their devices and backup materials.

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, or objection. The latest version of this policy will be kept on the BitSend site.

Children and international use

BitSend is not directed to children under 13, or the minimum legal age in your jurisdiction. The app may be used globally and network interactions may involve infrastructure outside your country.
