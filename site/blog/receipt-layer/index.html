The receipt layer should feel invisible

A receipt system should deepen trust after the send without making the send itself feel heavier.

Back to Blog Related Research
Design note

People want clarity after money moves.

A payment receipt is not just a nice extra. It is part of how people decide whether they trust the system they just used. They want a record, a time, an amount, and a clear sense of what happened.

Design rule

Do not make the receipt the bottleneck.

BitSend treats the receipt layer as something that becomes richer after the payment, not something that must fully finish before the payment feels done. That is the difference between support and friction.

The best receipt flow feels almost invisible at the moment of action, then more useful when the user comes back later.
