The payment moment is the product

Why BitSend starts from the real in-person exchange instead of treating that moment as a side effect of later chain screens.

Back to Blog Related Research
Product note

The real exchange comes first.

Most crypto interfaces are built as if the important event is the final on-chain state. BitSend takes a different view. For a nearby payment, the most important event is that two people were together and completed the exchange clearly.

That means the product has to prioritize recognition, confidence, and speed in the moment. The chain result still matters, but it is supporting evidence, not the only thing the user experiences as the payment.

What changes

The interface becomes shorter, calmer, and more direct.

Once you decide the in-person moment is central, you stop designing like a control panel. You reduce setup noise. You make confirmation clearer. You let the app finish the heavy online work later without making the user feel blocked.
